Create a request

Check the data you need for creating a request.
Important: Fill out a request form for at least three months to one year before the actual analysis.
Tip: The options available on the New request screen depend on your defined settings. Some values may already be filled in for your convenience. To change a value, click .
  1. To open the Requests list, go to the left blue menu and click Requests.
  2. At the right top of the table, click > Create.
  3. In the Request Name field, enter a meaningful title to easily retrieve the request from the list.
  4. In the Request info tile, specify administrative info needed to plan the analysis.
    Important: Be aware that Budget numbers (TimeTell codes) can only be added or adjusted by admins.
  5. In the Tissue info tile, specify all plant-related info the lab needs for a correct analysis.
  6. In the Service info tile, specify service-specific parameters.
  7. In the Remarks tile, type any additional remarks for the lab and click Preplan.
    Important: Additional information can be added via the conversation functionality as well. However, this functionality is meant for customers to discuss among themselves during the requesting process and not to add specifications for lab analysts.
    Your request appears in the Requests list with a preplanned status. Until a request is submitted, all info remains adaptable, except for crop and tissue type. These field are linked to the sample container generator module. You cannot change the crop and tissue type once a container is created. BITS cargo is prepared once the sample container generator module is finished.

Data for creating a request

To fill in a request, MARqeT will ask specific request information. This includes general administrative info (Request info), plant-related info (Tissue info) and analysis specific info (Service info).

Request info

Field Explanation
Request Name Meaningful title to easily retrieve the request from the list.
Addressed lab

The lab that will execute the analysis you request.
Competence group

The department of the organization you belong to.

Note: Competence groups are managed in the vocabulary service ontology.
Budget number

TimeTell code for downstream invoicing.
Submitter Works on behalf of the customer and submits the request. Enter your own name.
Important: The submitter acts as the single point of contact (SPOC) between the lab and the customer.
Customer Is the reference for a specific request and is responsible for the business process and budget. Enter the name of the person in charge of your competence group.
Contacts Additional people who will receive a notification upon request completion because they are interested in the request results.
Expected arrival date

The date by which all sample containers should have arrived at the lab.

Tip: Sample containers can be sent to the lab in batches.
Estimated deadline

The date on which the results of the analysis are reported back to the customer.

Note: The actual deadline date may vary slightly depending on the capacity of the lab and the type of analysis. The lab will always contact the customer via the conversation functionality if any problems arise regarding planning. It is the responsibility of the submitter to adjust the deadline date.

Tissue info

Field Explanation
Crop

The name of the crop you request an analysis on, for example, wheat, cotton or oilseed.

Note: Crops are managed in the crop service ontology.
Estimated # of sample containers A rough estimate of the number of sample containers (96-well plates or one position containers) you will submit to the lab.
Tip: This number is meant to reserve lab capacity.
Note: The number of containers will define your service type options.
Geographical source The geographical location your sample originates from, for example, Mexico, Belgium or Hawaii.
Note: Geographical sources are managed in the location service ontology.

Service info

Field Explanation
Estimated # of markers / targets An estimated number of markers you want to analyze.
Tip: This number is meant to reserve lab capacity.
